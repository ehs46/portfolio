Phase 1 Course Goals


1. Foster the creative habits of mind

I like to think I am an ''outside the box'' thinker. I tend to follow a spark in my mind and often am never fully satisfied with the outcome it brings me- maybe thats my OCD diagnosis. All in all, out of these goals I believe this one is my strongest natural ability.

2. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

This class so far has taught me much about patience! I have no background in code, nor have I ever been interested in it.. until now. I have collaborated well with my peers by asking questions, and as a learner. This is relatively new role for me; art has come pretty naturally to me in life, however, this whole coding thing has not! I am excited every time I see a new post on canvas to check out the work my peers have posted, to see what possiblities I haven't discovered yet. I have also gotten along well with my classmates as I feel like some of us non coders relate to one another well, helping eachother and asking questions.

3. Think deeply about craft.

When Dr.Quigley told us about craft in art I never really developed a definition of this in my head. I think about engaging in my work, taking an actual interest in the art I am creating. In my opinion, I have done this well. I acknowledge all the time and effort it has taken to learn different pieces of code (and not just vibe coding!) but most importantly, I am engaged and enjoying the work that I am doing in this class. I appreciate the knowledge and interest that Dr. Quigley has on these subjects and that keeps me curious.

4. Engage in sustained research and skills development.

In terms of research, I think most of my learning has been through our class readings. My favorite reading we have been assigned was Platos Allegory of the Cave, which I had never read before this class. I think I have also made strides in progress of my coding abilities, granted, they were little to none before this class. While I have been experimenting the most with vibe coding, I have been able to figure out everything with Github pretty seemlessly. Additionally, I have been able to further develop my skills in Premiere Pro, which I am super excited about, thanks to youtube and Dr. Quigleys instructions.

5. Learn to present your work

I am not a public speaker. There is some peace of mind that presenting my work means I don't have to do to much talking in front of the class. Still, I think it helps to face your fears, right? So I have been making an attempt to share my work with my peers or even in front of the class when the opportunity presents itself.
